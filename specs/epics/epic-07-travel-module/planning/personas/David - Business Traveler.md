## ðŸŽ¨ Design Implications

### UI/UX Preferences

**Professional Aesthetic**:
- Clean, minimal, dark mode preferred
- No playful animations or consumer-focused language
- Data-dense but scannable (he processes info quickly)

**Speed & Efficiency**:
- Keyboard shortcuts for everything (power user)
- Batch operations ("Export all Tokyo recommendations")
- Quick capture (record with 1 tap, stop with 1 tap)

**Mobile-First, Desktop Enhancement**:
- 90% of usage on iPhone
- Desktop for monthly expense review, trip planning
- Apple Watch glances: "Recording...", "3 new tips"

**Integration Philosophy**:
- Don't replace existing tools, enhance them
- Export to where he already works (Expensify, Instagram, Calendar)
- API access for custom Zapier workflows

### Feature Priorities (Ranked)

1. **Seamless AirPods recording** (no phone manipulation needed)
2. **AI recommendation extraction** with GPS auto-tagging
3. **City-based filtering** for repeat visit planning
4. **Expense report export** (CSV with all required fields)
5. **Instagram caption generation** from transcript highlights
6. **Calendar integration** (add restaurants as events)
7. **Multi-language support** (Spanish, Japanese, French conversations)
8. **Recommendation scoring**: Track great vs. mediocre tips
9. **Social proof aggregation**: "5 people recommended this place"
10. **Business contact linking**: Connect conversations to CRM
11. **Offline mode**: Record on flights, sync on landing
12. **Smart reminders**: "Restaurant X is open tonight, you're 0.5mi away"

### Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)

**Workflow Disruption**:
- Requiring phone interaction during conversations
- Manual tagging/categorization (needs to be automatic)
- Desktop-first features (he's rarely at laptop)
- Slow processing (needs results within hours for same-day planning)

**Consumer-Focused Design**:
- Playful or "fun" language (he's using for work efficiency)
- Social features (doesn't want to share publicly)
- Gamification (points, badges, etc.â€”waste of screen space)

**Technical Limitations**:
- WiFi dependency (needs offline recording)
- Platform lock-in (uses iPhone + Mac, may switch)
- Proprietary formats (wants standard exports: CSV, JSON, iCal)
- No API/automation options (he builds Zapier workflows)

**Business Risks**:
- Privacy leaks (client conversations are confidential)
- No GDPR/compliance features
- Unclear data retention policies
- Can't delete sensitive recordings easily
