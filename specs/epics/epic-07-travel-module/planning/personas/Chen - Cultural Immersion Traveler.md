   - Wants to understand deeper meaning of literary allusions locals use
   - **Comprehension**: Feels like he's missing 30% of every conversation
   - **Integration**: Remains "foreign student" instead of culturally integrated

   - Learns words in context but forgets context later
   - Writes words phonetically in notebook, pronunciation is wrong
   - No way to review actual usage by native speakers
   - Anki flashcards lack real-world context

## ðŸŽ¨ Design Implications

### UI/UX Preferences

**Academic Aesthetic**:
- Clean, text-focused design (not flashy)
- Data-rich displays (analytics, progress charts)
- Search-first architecture (academic needs findability)
- Export-friendly (standard formats, citations)

**Language Learner Priorities**:
- Audio playback with speed control
- Side-by-side: Original audio + transcript
- Highlight unknown words for quick lookup
- Create flashcard with one tap

**Privacy & Ethics**:
- Clear consent management (recording others)
- Easy deletion of sensitive content
- No cloud requirement (offline-first)
- Export control (who can access what)

**Mobile + Desktop Harmony**:
- Record on phone (daily life)
- Analyze on laptop (research, serious study)
- Seamless sync between devices
- Offline-first, sync when available

### Feature Priorities (Ranked)

1. **Accurate multi-language transcription** (Mandarin, English, Spanish, Catalan mixed)
2. **Speaker identification** (tag who said what)
3. **Anki flashcard export** with audio clips + context
4. **Error pattern tracking** (analyze corrections over time)
5. **Bibliography extraction** (books, articles, authors mentioned)
6. **Searchable archive** (find conversations by topic, person, date)
7. **Quote export with timestamps** (citeable academic sources)
8. **Vocabulary analytics** (growth, frequency, retention)
9. **Idiom/cultural reference flagging**
10. **Progress tracking** (fluency metrics over time)
11. **Offline mode** (record + review without internet)
12. **Standard format exports** (TXT, MD, CSV for analysis)
13. **Zotero/Notion integration**
14. **Privacy controls** (consent, deletion, encryption)

### Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)

**Academic Deal-Breakers**:
- No export options (needs data ownership)
- Proprietary formats (can't analyze in R/Python)
- Cloud-only (privacy concerns for research)
- No citation-ready output (needs timestamps, speaker IDs)
- Can't organize by project/topic (dissertation has chapters)

**Language Learning Failures**:
- Text-only (needs audio for pronunciation)
- No spaced repetition (passive review is useless)
- Can't track corrections (just transcription isn't enough)
- No progress metrics (wants data-driven learning)
- Auto-translation without context (he needs to learn, not just understand)

**Privacy & Ethics**:
- No consent management (recording others is ethically complex)
- Auto-upload to cloud (university IRB would reject)
- Social sharing defaults (academic research is confidential)
- No encryption option (dissertation data must be secure)

**Workflow Disruption**:
- Desktop-first features (he lives on mobile)
- Requires internet for basic functions (studies in libraries)
- Slow processing (wants same-day review)
- Can't integrate with existing tools (Anki, Notion, Zotero)
- Manual organization (no time for elaborate tagging)

**Student Budget Reality**:
- Expensive subscription (>$15/month is hard)
- Per-minute transcription costs (conversation-based learning adds up)
- No academic/student discount (assumes commercial pricing)
- Required hardware purchases (can't afford new equipment)
