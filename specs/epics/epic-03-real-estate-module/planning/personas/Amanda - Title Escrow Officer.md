## ðŸ˜“ Pain Points & Frustrations

### Current Closing Challenges
1. **Multi-Party Coordination Chaos**
   - Closings involve 5-8 people: buyer, seller, buyer agent, seller agent, lender rep, sometimes attorney
   - Everyone has different recollections of what was agreed to
   - "You said the $1,200 HOA special assessment would be seller's responsibility!"
   - Amanda's handwritten notes can't capture everything when 8 people are talking

2. **Post-Closing Disputes**
   - 3-5 disputes/year: "That's not what we agreed to at closing"
   - Example: Seller claims buyer agreed to take refrigerator "as-is," buyer claims seller agreed to replace broken ice maker
   - No clear record of verbal side agreements made during closing
   - Disputes cost $2K-$10K to resolve (legal fees, re-opening escrow, reputation damage)

3. **Complex Title Issues**
   - Title defects discovered during escrow: liens, easements, boundary disputes, clouds on title
   - Negotiates resolution verbally with all parties
   - Weeks later: "Wait, did we agree the seller would pay off the $8K mechanic's lien or the buyer?"
   - Amanda's notes from that 45-min conference call are sketchy

4. **Closing Disclosure (CD) Accuracy**
   - CD must reflect all agreed-upon terms, costs, who pays what
   - Verbal agreements at pre-closing walkthrough don't make it to CD
   - Last-minute changes ("Buyer will cover the $500 HOA transfer fee") get missed in chaos
   - Errors on CD delay closing, require re-signing, stress for all parties

5. **Wire Fraud Risk**
   - Escrow wiring fraud is rampant ($200M+ stolen annually nationwide)
   - Amanda verbally confirms wiring instructions with buyer by phone
   - No record of what was said: "I called you and confirmed the account number!"
   - If fraud occurs, Amanda's company is liable (insurance claims require proof of process)

6. **Training New Staff**
   - Closings are complex, each one unique
   - New escrow assistants struggle to learn nuances
   - Amanda wants to share transcripts of complex closings as training examples
   - "Listen to how I handled the survey dispute on the Johnson closing"

### Technology Pain Points
- Uses title production software (SoftPro, RamQuest) - data entry heavy, no voice input
- Handwritten notes during closings (can't type while managing 8 people)
- Notes are fragmented: "Buyer pays HOA" - which buyer? which fee?
- No systematic way to document verbal side agreements
- Post-closing disputes become he-said/she-said with no proof

## ðŸŽ“ Typical Use Cases

### Closing Meeting Workflow (Current State - Without Transcript Parser)
1. **Pre-Closing Prep (2 hours before)**:
   - Reviews file, prepares HUD-1/Closing Disclosure
   - Prints 50+ pages of closing documents
   - Anticipates issues: "Survey shows fence encroaches 6 inches on neighbor's property - buyer and seller need to agree on resolution"
2. **Closing Meeting (60-90 min, conference room)**:
   - Buyer, seller, both agents, lender rep present (6 people)
   - Amanda explains each document: "This is the Closing Disclosure, showing all costs. Buyer, you're bringing $78,453.22 via wire. Seller, you'll net $201,340.18 after payoffs."
   - Sidebar negotiation: "Buyer wants refrigerator ice maker fixed. Seller offers $200 credit instead. Agreed?"
   - Another issue: "Survey shows fence encroachment. Seller agrees to move fence post-closing within 30 days or buyer can deduct $800 from escrow."
   - Amanda scribbles notes: "ice maker $200 credit," "fence 30 days or $800"
   - Forgets exact wording of fence agreement
3. **Post-Closing (same day, 2 hours)**:
   - Types notes into SoftPro from memory
   - Disperses funds, records deed, sends final paperwork
   - Hopes all verbal side agreements were captured accurately
4. **2 Weeks Later (dispute call)**:
   - Seller's agent: "Seller moved the fence, but buyer is demanding the $800 from escrow anyway. Buyer says seller agreed to move fence AND give $800 credit."
   - Amanda: "That's not what I remember. Let me check my notes... 'fence 30 days or $800.'"
   - Seller's agent: "That's ambiguous. Did 'or' mean both or one?"
   - Amanda has no recording, no proof of exact wording
   - 3 hours of phone calls, potential legal dispute

### Closing Meeting Workflow (Ideal - With Transcript Parser + Local AI)
1. **Pre-Closing Prep (2 hours before)**:
   - Same file review and prep
   - Sets up Transcript Parser in conference room (local AI for privacy)
   - Announces to all parties: "I'll be recording today's closing for documentation purposes. Everyone okay with that?" (all consent)
2. **Closing Meeting (60-90 min, conference room)**:
   - Transcript Parser records entire meeting with speaker identification (6 people)
   - Amanda explains Closing Disclosure naturally, knows all disclosures are captured
   - Sidebar negotiation: "Buyer wants refrigerator ice maker fixed. Seller offers $200 credit instead. Buyer, do you accept?" "Yes, agreed."
   - Survey issue: "Seller, you agree to move fence within 30 days of closing. If not completed, buyer may deduct $800 from escrow holdback. Everyone clear?" "Yes, agreed."
   - All verbal side agreements captured with speaker attribution
3. **Post-Closing (same day, 30 minutes)**:
   - Reviews AI transcript summary:
     - Main terms: Purchase price, closing costs, wire amounts
     - Side agreements: Ice maker $200 credit to buyer, Fence relocation 30 days OR $800 deduction
   - Exports key sections to SoftPro notes
   - Sends transcript excerpt to buyer and seller: "Here's our fence agreement from closing"
4. **2 Weeks Later (potential dispute avoided)**:
   - Seller's agent calls: "Seller moved the fence, but buyer is claiming they're still owed $800."
   - Amanda searches transcript: "fence" - finds exact quote: "Seller agrees to move fence within 30 days. If not completed, buyer may deduct $800. Seller, do you agree?" Seller: "Yes, I agree."
   - Amanda sends transcript excerpt to buyer's agent: "Here's what was agreed. Seller moved fence, so no $800 deduction."
   - Dispute resolved in 5 minutes, no legal fees

### Specific Scenarios
- **Title defect resolution**: Records conference call with buyer, seller, and title underwriter about lien resolution - clear record of who agreed to pay
- **Lender last-minute changes**: Lender changes closing costs 2 hours before closing, Amanda records conversation with buyer acknowledging changes
- **Wire fraud prevention**: Records phone call confirming wiring instructions with buyer, timestamped proof for insurance/compliance
- **Remote closings**: Records RON (remote online notarization) sessions, supplements e-signature with transcript
- **Training new staff**: Shares transcripts of complex closings with escrow assistants as learning examples
- **Post-closing audits**: Provides transcripts to title underwriter or auditor as proof of proper procedure

## ðŸŽ¨ Design Implications

### UI/UX Preferences
- **Local AI (Ollama) REQUIRED** - Cannot send legal/financial data to cloud
- **Multi-speaker identification** - Label each speaker (Buyer, Seller, Buyer Agent, etc.)
- **Conference room setup** - Laptop-based recording, multiple microphones (8 people around table)
- **Real-time verification** - Quick glance to confirm recording is active
- **Post-meeting summary** - AI extracts key agreements, dollar amounts, action items
- **SoftPro integration** - Export notes directly into title software

### Feature Priorities (Ranked)
1. **Local AI processing (Ollama)** - Non-negotiable for legal/financial compliance
2. **Multi-speaker identification** - Must know who said what (6-8 people)
3. **SoftPro integration** - Auto-populate closing notes from transcript
4. **Search by dollar amount** - "$5,000 credit," "$800 escrow holdback"
5. **Secure sharing** - Send excerpt to buyer/seller post-closing (encrypted)
6. **Template extraction** - AI pulls: purchase price, closing costs, side agreements, wire amounts
7. **Retention policy management** - Auto-archive after 7 years (state law)

### Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)
- **Cloud-only transcription** (DEALBREAKER - legal/financial data cannot go to cloud)
- **Mobile-only interface** (closings are desktop-based in conference rooms)
- **No speaker identification** (multi-party closings require attribution)
- **Poor audio quality** (8 people talking, some soft-spoken, must capture all)
- **No export functionality** (must integrate with SoftPro or export structured data)
- **Complex setup** (needs simple one-button start for busy pre-closing prep)
