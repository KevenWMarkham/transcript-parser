## ðŸ˜“ Pain Points & Frustrations

### Current Compliance Challenges
1. **Documentation Overload**
   - TILA/RESPA regulations require detailed records of all borrower interactions
   - Manual notes in Encompass: "Discussed rate options, borrower prefers 30-yr fixed" (incomplete, time-consuming)
   - Compliance audits ask: "Did you disclose the APR? Did borrower acknowledge?" - hard to prove from vague notes
   - Fear of violations: $10K-$50K fines per violation, career-ending for serious infractions

2. **Memory Limitations**
   - Initial consultation: 45-60 minutes covering employment, income, assets, debts, credit, property, loan options
   - Remembers 60% by the time she updates Encompass that evening
   - Borrower calls 3 days later: "You said my rate would be 3.625%, right?" - Jennifer isn't sure, depends on lock date
   - Forgets critical borrower disclosures: "Did I explain the prepayment penalty on the 15-year option?"

3. **Borrower Disputes**
   - "You said I was approved!" vs. "I said pre-qualified, pending full underwriting"
   - "You quoted 3.5% with no points!" vs. "I said 3.5% with 1 point OR 3.75% with zero points"
   - No written record of verbal conversations, becomes he-said-she-said
   - Lost deal in 2022: Borrower sued claiming Jennifer misrepresented costs - settled for $15K (no proof of what was actually said)

4. **Client Communication Gaps**
   - Explains complex loan terms verbally (APR vs. interest rate, points, closing costs, PMI, escrow)
   - Borrowers forget 70% by the time they review Loan Estimate
   - Calls Jennifer with questions already answered in initial consultation
   - Jennifer re-explains the same concepts 3-4 times per borrower (inefficient)

5. **Realtor Relationship Management**
   - Agents refer clients but expect fast service
   - Agent: "You said pre-approval in 24 hours" vs. Jennifer: "I said 2-3 business days pending doc review"
   - No record of agent conversations about buyer qualifications, timing expectations
   - Miscommunication damages referral relationships

### Technology Pain Points
- Encompass LOS (loan origination system): Clunky note-taking, no voice input
- Manually types conversation summaries (8+ hrs/week)
- Uses iPhone Notes for quick reminders (not secure, not compliant)
- Email follow-ups to clients (time-consuming, borrowers don't read long emails)
- No systematic way to capture verbal disclosures for compliance

## ðŸŽ“ Typical Use Cases

### Loan Consultation Workflow (Current State - Without Transcript Parser)
1. **Initial Borrower Consultation (60 min at coffee shop)**:
   - Reviews borrower's employment, income, assets, debts, credit situation
   - Explains loan options: FHA vs. Conventional, 30-yr vs. 15-yr, rate vs. points
   - Discusses rates (today's rate 3.75%, locks for 30 days, subject to credit/appraisal)
   - Scribbles notes on pad: "John Doe, software engineer $120K, wife nurse $65K, $50K down, looking at $450K home"
2. **After Meeting (30 min back at office)**:
   - Types detailed notes into Encompass from memory and messy handwritten notes
   - Forgets 40% of conversation: "Did he say he had student loans paid off or still paying?"
   - Emails borrower: "Thank you for meeting, here's what we discussed..." (2-paragraph summary, incomplete)
3. **3 Days Later (borrower calls with questions)**:
   - "You said my rate would be 3.625%, but the Loan Estimate says 3.75%?"
   - Jennifer doesn't remember exact quote, checks notes (vague)
   - Explains: "Market rates changed, and it depends on final credit score" (borrower frustrated, feels bait-and-switched)
4. **Audit 6 Months Later**:
   - Examiner: "Show me documentation of your TILA disclosures to borrower #4782"
   - Jennifer: "Um, here are my notes in Encompass: 'Discussed APR and closing costs'" (insufficient detail)
   - Examiner: "Did borrower acknowledge understanding? Do you have signed disclosure?"
   - Jennifer: "Well, we talked about it..." (no proof, potential violation flagged)

### Loan Consultation Workflow (Ideal - With Transcript Parser + Local AI)
1. **Initial Borrower Consultation (60 min at coffee shop)**:
   - Starts Transcript Parser (local AI mode for PII protection): "Loan consultation, John and Mary Doe, file #2024-1847"
   - Discusses all loan details naturally, knows everything is recorded securely
   - No note-taking during meeting (fully present, builds rapport)
2. **After Meeting (5 min in car)**:
   - Reviews AI-generated summary:
     - Employment: John software engineer $120K/yr, Mary nurse $65K/yr
     - Assets: $50K down payment, $30K reserves
     - Debts: Car loan $350/mo, student loans paid off last year
     - Property: $450K target, first-time buyer, Seattle area
     - Loan: FHA 30-year fixed, discussed rates 3.75-3.875% range based on final credit
     - Disclosures: Explained APR vs. rate, points, PMI, closing costs, borrower acknowledged understanding
   - One-click export to Encompass (auto-populates borrower file)
3. **3 Days Later (borrower calls with questions)**:
   - "You said my rate would be 3.625%?"
   - Jennifer searches transcript: "rate" - finds exact quote: "Today's rates are 3.75-3.875% for FHA 30-year, depending on credit score and lock date. Rates can change daily."
   - "Let me pull up our conversation. I quoted 3.75-3.875% depending on your final credit score. Your score came in at 720, so you qualified for 3.75%. Would you like me to send you the exact quote from our meeting?"
   - Borrower: "Oh, I must have misunderstood. Thanks for clarifying."
4. **Audit 6 Months Later**:
   - Examiner: "Show me documentation of TILA disclosures for borrower #4782"
   - Jennifer: "Here's the full transcript of our consultation. See timestamp 14:32 where I explain APR calculation and timestamp 18:45 where borrower confirms understanding."
   - Examiner: "Perfect. Well documented."

### Specific Scenarios
- **Rate lock conversation**: Records exact rate quoted, lock period, expiration - protects against disputes
- **Credit explanation**: Borrower explains past late payments, Jennifer has transcript for underwriter notes
- **Realtor coordination**: Records agent's timeline expectations, buyer qualification questions
- **Refinance consultation**: Transcribes break-even analysis, costs vs. savings, borrower decision rationale
- **Pre-approval discussion**: Captures borrower's max budget, monthly payment comfort level, property preferences
- **Compliance audit**: Provides transcripts as proof of regulatory disclosures and borrower acknowledgment

## ðŸŽ¨ Design Implications

### UI/UX Preferences
- **Local AI (Ollama) REQUIRED** - Cannot send PII/financial data to cloud
- **Bank-grade security** - Encryption, access controls, audit logs
- **Simple recording workflow** - One-tap start, no complex setup
- **Compliance-focused UI** - Tag disclosures, flag important statements
- **Export to Encompass** - Integration with LOS (or at minimum CSV export)
- **Redaction tools** - Easy removal of SSNs, account numbers before sharing

### Feature Priorities (Ranked)
1. **Local AI processing (Ollama)** - Non-negotiable for PII compliance
2. **Encompass LOS integration** - Auto-populate borrower notes from transcript
3. **Compliance tagging** - Mark TILA disclosures, borrower acknowledgments
4. **Secure sharing** - Send transcript excerpts to borrowers (encrypted links)
5. **Search by topic** - "rate locks," "TILA disclosures," "borrower questions"
6. **Redaction tools** - Remove sensitive data (SSN, account numbers) before sharing
7. **Retention policy management** - Auto-delete after 7 years (TILA requirement)

### Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)
- **Cloud-only transcription** (DEALBREAKER - cannot send PII to cloud)
- **No export functionality** (must integrate with Encompass or export CSV)
- **Visible recording indicators** (breaks rapport during sensitive financial discussions)
- **Poor security** (bank compliance requires encryption, access logs, audit trails)
- **No redaction tools** (can't share transcripts with SSNs, account numbers exposed)
- **Complex setup** (needs one-tap start for client meetings)
