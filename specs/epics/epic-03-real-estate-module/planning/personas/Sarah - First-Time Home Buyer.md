## ðŸŽ¯ Goals & Motivations

### Home Buying Goals
- **Find the perfect first home** - 2-bedroom condo or small house in East Austin
- **Make a confident decision** - First major purchase, wants to avoid buyer's remorse
- **Stay within budget** - Pre-approved for $350K, wants to keep monthly payment under $2,500
- **Close within 3 months** - Current lease ends in 90 days, needs to time move

### Personal Goals

- Find a home that supports her lifestyle (walkable to coffee shops, dog-friendly)

### Why She Needs Transcript Parser
- **Property confusion** - After seeing 18 properties, can't remember which had which features
- **Decision paralysis** - Overwhelming amount of information from Lisa, inspectors, mortgage broker
- **Partner misalignment** - Best friend (co-decision maker) can't attend all showings, needs to share details
- **Detail tracking** - Lisa mentions important things ("HOA covers roof," "seller might negotiate") but Sarah forgets
- **Comparison struggles** - Needs to compare properties side-by-side but notes are incomplete
- **Question tracking** - Thinks of questions after showings, can't remember if they were answered

## ðŸ˜“ Pain Points & Frustrations

### Current Home Search Challenges
1. **Property Overload**
   - Viewed 18 properties in 2 months (some twice)
   - Properties blur together: "Wait, which one had the updated kitchen?"
   - Confused about which features belong to which house
   - Can't remember Lisa's specific comments about each property

2. **Note-Taking Struggles**
   - Takes photos but forgets context ("Why did I take a photo of this closet?")
   - Scribbles notes on phone while touring (awkward, misses what Lisa is saying)
   - Notes are fragmented: "granite counters" - but which house?
   - Friend asks questions she can't answer: "Did the $320K one have covered parking?"

3. **Information Overwhelm**
   - Lisa shares critical details verbally: market trends, neighborhood insights, negotiation strategies
   - Inspector explains issues during walkthrough (electrical panel, roof condition) - forgets 70% by evening
   - Mortgage broker discusses rates and terms (confusing jargon) - can't recall specifics later
   - HOA rules, utility costs, property tax estimates - all verbal, all forgotten

4. **Decision-Making Anxiety**
   - Biggest purchase of her life, terrified of making wrong choice
   - Compares properties from memory (unreliable)
   - Partner (best friend Sarah consults) wasn't at 12 of the 18 showings - hard to share context
   - Needs to re-visit properties multiple times because forgot details

5. **Communication Gaps**
   - Lisa mentions "seller is motivated" or "comparable sold for $15K more" - Sarah forgets specifics
   - Questions arise days later: "Did Lisa say the AC was new or just serviced?"
   - Can't remember if specific concerns (like noise from highway) were addressed
   - Misses subtle cues from Lisa about property value or issues

### Technology Pain Points
- Uses iPhone Notes app (disorganized, hard to search)
- Takes 200+ photos across properties (no organization, context lost)
- Tries to record Lisa with voice memos (feels rude, rarely reviews 45-min files)
- Pinterest board for "dream home features" doesn't connect to actual properties seen
- Spreadsheet with property addresses (no detail, just prices and bed/bath counts)

## ðŸŽ“ Typical Use Cases

### Property Showing Workflow (Current State - Without Transcript Parser)
1. **Before Showing**: Reviews Zillow listing, creates mental checklist of questions
2. **During Showing** (awkward multitasking):
   - Takes 15-20 photos per property
   - Tries to listen to Lisa while typing notes on phone
   - Misses half of Lisa's insights while photographing
   - Feels rude recording with voice memos, rarely does it
3. **After Showing** (in car, rushed):
   - Adds property to spreadsheet (basic info only)
   - Types fragmented notes: "nice kitchen, small backyard, Lisa said something about roof?"
   - Forgets 60% of conversation by the time she gets home
4. **That Evening** (decision-making):
   - Looks at photos, can't remember context
   - Texts Lisa with questions she forgot to ask
   - Friend asks "What did Lisa think?" - Sarah can't remember exactly
   - Spends 2 hours trying to reconstruct the showing from photos and memory

### Property Showing Workflow (Ideal - With Transcript Parser)
1. **Before Showing**: Lisa shares Transcript Parser link, Sarah opens app
2. **During Showing** (fully present):
   - Taps "Start Recording" on phone (Lisa consented)
   - Focuses entirely on Lisa and property, no note-taking
   - Asks all questions naturally, knows everything is captured
   - Takes photos without needing to annotate context
3. **After Showing** (in car, 5 minutes):
   - Reviews AI summary: "3-bed, 2-bath, $315K, Lisa noted: excellent school district, seller motivated, roof is 8 years old, HOA $150/month"
   - Tags property: "Top 3" and "Great location"
   - Shares transcript link with friend for feedback
4. **That Evening** (confident decision-making):
   - Searches all transcripts for "HOA fees" - instant results across all 18 properties
   - Compares Lisa's comments side-by-side: "This is the best value I've seen" vs. "Overpriced for this neighborhood"
   - Reviews inspector's transcript about electrical panel concerns (remembered clearly)
   - Shares key excerpts with friend via text

### Specific Scenarios
- **Multiple showings in one day**: Keeps properties distinct by recording each separately with property address tags
- **Inspection walkthrough**: Records inspector's findings, searches transcript later for "foundation" or "plumbing"
- **Mortgage consultation**: Records loan officer's rate comparison, refers back when making offer decisions
- **Follow-up questions**: Searches Lisa's transcripts to verify what was said before texting new questions
- **Sharing with friend/partner**: Sends transcript excerpts instead of trying to summarize

### How Sarah Measures Success

## ðŸŽ¨ Design Implications

### UI/UX Preferences
- **Dead-simple interface** - Sarah is stressed, needs one-tap recording
- **Mobile-only workflow** - Never at a desktop during showings
- **Property organization** - Must tag/categorize properties easily
- **Search-first** - Needs to find specific details across all properties
- **Sharing-friendly** - Needs to share with friend, family, maybe co-buyer
- **Visual connection** - Link transcripts to photos taken at same time

### Feature Priorities (Ranked)
1. **One-tap recording with property tag** - "123 Oak St" automatically in transcript
2. **Search across all properties** - Find "updated kitchen" in all 18 transcripts
3. **AI summary per property** - 5-bullet-point summary of Lisa's key points
4. **Share transcript links** - Send to friend for feedback
5. **Comparison view** - Side-by-side transcripts of top 3 properties
6. **Tag/categorize** - "Top 3," "Pass," "Reconsider," "Need Second Showing"
7. **Highlight key quotes** - Pin Lisa's most important comments

### Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)
- Complex setup before each showing (needs instant start)
- Requiring Sarah to manually transcribe or edit (defeats the purpose)
- Desktop-only features (100% mobile workflow)
- Privacy concerns (needs clear consent from Lisa, inspector, etc.)
- Expensive premium features (on a tight budget)
- Technical jargon (she's not a real estate professional)
