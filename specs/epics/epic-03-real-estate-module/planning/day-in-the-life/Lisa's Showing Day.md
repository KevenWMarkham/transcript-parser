### 7:00 AM - Morning Routine & Prep
- **Context**: Saturday = busiest day, 5 showings scheduled
- **Mindset**: Excited but overwhelmed - must remember details for each client
- **Prep**: Reviews client notes from previous showings while daughter eats breakfast

### 8:30 AM - First Showing: Johnson Family (First-time buyers, $400K budget)
**Location**: 3BR/2BA ranch in Hyde Park, $385K

**Without Transcript Parser**:
- Arrives early, unlocks house, turns on lights
- Johnsons arrive, excited and nervous
- Lisa shows property, points out features
- Mrs. Johnson: "I love the natural light in the kitchen, but the carpet in the bedrooms needs to go"
- Mr. Johnson: "The backyard is perfect for our dog, but I wish the garage was bigger"
- Lisa tries to remember these comments while showing master bath
- Forgets Mrs. Johnson's comment about wanting a home office space
- After showing, sits in car, frantically writes notes: "liked kitchen light... something about carpet..."
- Realizes she forgot half the conversation
- Texts husband: "What did your wife say about the office?"

**With Transcript Parser (Cloud AI)**:
- Opens Transcript Parser: "New Recording - Johnson Family - 123 Oak St"
- Taps "Start" as family arrives
- Shows property naturally, focused on building rapport
- Mrs. Johnson: "I love the natural light, but the carpet needs to go"
- Lisa: "Great point about the carpet - let me show you the closet space"
- Mr. Johnson mentions backyard for dog, smaller garage concern
- Lisa asks follow-up questions, continues conversation naturally
- No note-taking - phone recording in her pocket
- After showing (in car, 3 minutes):
  - Opens app, sees AI summary:
    - **Loved**: Natural kitchen light, backyard size, quiet street
    - **Concerns**: Carpet condition, garage size, no dedicated office space
    - **Next steps**: Find similar homes with hardwood + 2-car garage
- **Total time**: 3 min vs. 15 min manual note reconstruction

**With Transcript Parser + Meta Ray-Ban Glasses (Discrete)**:
- Wears Meta Ray-Ban Smart Glasses (look like regular sunglasses)
- Starts recording with tap on temple (discrete, no phone visible)
- **Benefit**: Hands-free - can gesture, point, open doors naturally
- **Benefit**: Glasses capture spatial audio - remembers which room comments were made in
- **Cultural benefit**: No phone visible = more professional, focused on clients
- After showing: Has full transcript + spatial context
- Reviews summary while driving to next showing

## üåû Afternoon (12:00 PM - 5:00 PM)

### 12:00 PM - Lunch Break (30 min)
**Without Transcript Parser**:
- Grabs fast food, tries to write notes from morning showings
- Can't remember which family said what
- Stresses about sending follow-ups, puts it off

**With Transcript Parser**:
- Grabs lunch, opens app
- Reviews both morning summaries (5 min total)
- App auto-generates draft follow-up emails
- Edits slightly, sends from phone
- Both families receive personalized emails within 2 hours of showing
- Enjoys rest of lunch break

**With Transcript Parser + Local AI (Ollama - Privacy Mode)**:
Garcia family mentioned their **exact budget ($550K)** and that **Mr. Garcia just got promoted** (income increase). This is sensitive financial info.

1. **Privacy-conscious processing**:
   - Lisa has Ollama running on her iPhone (via Termux or local server)
   - Transcript processed locally - no cloud API call
   - **Privacy**: Client financial info never sent to Google/OpenAI
   - **Compliance**: Texas Real Estate Commission privacy rules followed
   - **Trust**: If client asks "did you record that?", Lisa can say "yes, but it stays on my phone, never goes to any cloud service"

2. **Why Lisa uses local AI**:
   - **Client privacy**: Financial details, divorce mention, job changes - sensitive info
   - **Compliance**: Real estate privacy regulations
   - **Trust**: Can honestly tell clients data is private
   - **Cost**: Free processing (vs. $0.05/showing to cloud AI) = $300/year savings
   - **Offline**: Some luxury properties in areas with no cell signal

## üí° Key Insights

### What Made the Difference
1. **Hands-free recording** - Focus on clients, not note-taking
2. **AI summaries** - 3 min review vs. 15 min note reconstruction
3. **Personalized follow-ups** - Clients feel heard and valued
4. **Action item extraction** - Never miss a client question
5. **Discrete recording (XR glasses)** - Professional, no phone distraction
6. **Local AI (Ollama)** - Privacy for sensitive client information
7. **Search across showings** - Find that "3BR with office" discussion from last week

### Feature Usage
- üìù **Transcription**: Every showing (100%)

## üéØ Implications for Design

### Must-Have Features (Based on Lisa's Day)
1. **One-tap recording start** - No time for complex setup before client arrives
2. **AI client preference extraction** - "Loved X, concerned about Y"
3. **Follow-up email templates** - Auto-draft personalized emails
4. **Action item extraction** - "Client asked about X, needs answer"
5. **Client comparison** - "Which clients wanted granite counters?"
6. **Discrete recording mode** - XR glasses or pocket recording
7. **Local AI option (Ollama)** - Privacy for sensitive client info
8. **CRM integration** - Auto-update Zillow/Realtor.com notes

### User Flow Priorities
1. Showing starts ‚Üí One-tap record ‚Üí Focus on client (< 5 sec)
2. Showing ends ‚Üí Review 2-min summary ‚Üí Send follow-up (< 5 min)
3. Search all clients ‚Üí Find preference ‚Üí Schedule targeted showing (< 2 min)

### Metrics to Track
- **Time to follow-up** - Goal: < 2 hours post-showing
- **Follow-up personalization** - Measure specific client mentions in email
- **Deal close rate** - Track improvement with Transcript Parser usage
