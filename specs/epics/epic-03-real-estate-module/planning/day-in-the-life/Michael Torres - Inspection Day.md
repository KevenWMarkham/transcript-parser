## üåÖ Morning: Inspection #1 - Pre-Purchase

### 8:45 AM - Arrival at 1423 Oak St

**Client**: Sarah Chen (first-time buyer) + Lisa Martinez (her agent)
**Property**: 3BR/2BA single-family, 1,850 sq ft, $315K purchase price

#### Without Transcript Parser ‚ùå
- Michael arrives, greets Sarah and Lisa
- Brings clipboard with pre-printed checklist
- Explains inspection process: "I'll spend 2-3 hours going through everything. I'll take photos and notes, then you'll get a written report in 24-48 hours."
- Sarah: "Can I follow you around and ask questions?"
- Michael: "Sure, but I'll be moving quickly and may miss some of your questions while I'm working."

**9:00 AM - Exterior Inspection** (45 min)
- Michael walks around property with clipboard
- Roof (viewed from ground): Shingles look worn, tries to estimate age
- Scribbles: "roof 20+ yrs?, curling SW corner"
- Can't write detail while holding ladder
- Climbs ladder to inspect roof closer - both hands needed for safety
- Sees granule loss, damaged flashing - can't take notes on ladder
- Comes down, tries to remember: "Was that north or south side flashing?"
- Scribbles: "flashing dmg, granules, maybe 3-5 yrs left"
- Foundation: Sees hairline crack, measures 6 feet long with tape measure
- Writes: "found crack 6ft, cosmetic?"
- Forgets to note location (east wall vs. west wall)

**10:00 AM - Interior Inspection** (75 min)
- HVAC: Opens panel, checks filter (dirty), tries to read model number
- Clipboard on floor, bends down to write: "filter dirty, 2015 Trane"
- Electrical panel: Opens, sees 3 double-tapped breakers
- Counts circuits, writes: "100A panel, 3 dbl taps circuits 4,7,12"
- Takes photo of panel with phone
- Plumbing: Checks under sinks, sees slow drain in guest bath
- Writes: "guest bath slow drain"
- Main bathroom tub: Soft spot in floor - possible water damage
- Scribbles: "soft floor main bath - water dmg?"
- Can't write more detail because hands are dirty from crawling

**11:30 AM - Attic Inspection** (30 min)
- Climbs into attic - hot, cramped, dusty
- Clipboard left below (too dirty up here)
- Sees inadequate insulation (R-19, should be R-38 for Austin)
- Moisture stains on decking near bathroom vent
- Structural framing looks good
- Climbs down - tries to remember everything
- Scribbles: "insul R-19 low, moist stains bath vent, frame ok"
- Forgets to note exact location of moisture stains

**12:00 PM - Client Walkthrough** (30 min)
- Michael explains major findings to Sarah and Lisa
- "The roof is at end of life, you'll need to replace it in 3-5 years, probably $8-12K"
- Sarah: "You said there was an electrical problem?"
- Michael: "Yeah, some double-tapped breakers - safety issue, electrician should fix"
- Sarah: "How much will that cost?"
- Michael: "Probably $150-300 depending on whether panel can handle tandem breakers"
- Sarah: "And what about that floor issue in the bathroom?"
- Michael: "Could be water damage from tub, need to investigate further, might be $500-2000 to repair"
- Sarah tries to take notes on phone, misses half of what Michael says
- Lisa records with voice memo but never transcribes it

**Evening (6:00 PM - 11:00 PM): Report Writing** (5 hours)
- Michael opens HomeGauge software
- Imports 213 photos from phone
- Tries to organize photos: "Which room is this water heater in?"
- Reads handwritten notes: "flashing dmg" - which side of roof?
- Expands shorthand from memory:
  - "roof 20+ yrs" becomes "Roof: Architectural shingles, approximately 20-25 years old based on visual assessment, showing moderate wear with granule loss and curling primarily on southwest exposure. Recommendation: Budget for replacement within 3-5 years. Estimated cost: $8,000-$12,000."
- Forgets some details: Was the soft floor in main bath or guest bath?
- Checks photos - bathroom photos don't show floor issue clearly
- Guesses based on vague memory
- Writes 42-page report, finishes at 11 PM
- **Accuracy**: 85% (forgot some details, guessed on others)
- **Quality**: Good, but gaps where memory failed
- **Time**: 5 hours
- **Exhaustion**: High

**9:00 AM - Exterior Inspection** (45 min, fully hands-free)
- **Roof** (on ladder, hands-free dictation):
  - "Roof: Architectural shingles, appears to be original 1998 installation, now 26 years old. The manufacturer rating was likely 25 years. Currently showing moderate to severe wear. Southwest exposure shows significant granule loss - approximately 40-50% of protective granules missing. Shingles are curling and becoming brittle. Flashing around the chimney on the north side is pulling away from the masonry - gap of approximately 1-2 inches. This is allowing potential water intrusion. Recommendation: Roof replacement needed within 1-3 years. This is not an emergency but should be prioritized. Estimated cost for full roof replacement: $8,000 to $12,000 depending on shingle quality and contractor."
  - **Benefit**: Exact details captured while on ladder, both hands free for safety

- **Foundation** (kneeling, tape measure in hand):
  - "Foundation: Poured concrete slab. Identified a hairline crack on the east exterior wall, approximately 6 feet long, running vertically from ground level to 3 feet above grade. Crack width approximately 1/16 inch - within normal settlement range. No active moisture, no displacement, no step-cracking. Assessment: Cosmetic crack due to normal concrete curing and settlement, not structural. Recommendation: Monitor annually, seal with exterior caulk to prevent moisture intrusion. Cost: $50-100 DIY, $150-250 if hiring contractor."

**10:00 AM - Interior Inspection** (75 min, hands-free)
- **Electrical Panel** (hands free to open panel, probe circuits):
  - "Electrical: Main service panel located in garage. 100-amp service. Panel is Siemens brand, appears to be original to home. Identified three double-tapped breakers - this is a safety code violation. Specifically: Circuit 4 (master bedroom outlets), Circuit 7 (kitchen appliances), and Circuit 12 (exterior outlets) each have two wires under a single breaker terminal. This can cause overheating and fire risk. Recommendation: Licensed electrician should separate these circuits or install approved tandem breakers if panel capacity allows. Estimated cost: $150 to $300. Priority: Medium - should be addressed within 30 days of closing."

- **Plumbing** (under sink, hands dirty, still dictating):
  - "Plumbing: Guest bathroom sink drainage is slow - tested with running water for 60 seconds, drain time approximately 2 minutes to empty bowl. Likely partial clog or venting issue. Checked P-trap, no visible leaks but trap is corroded. Main bathroom: Detected soft spot in floor adjacent to bathtub, approximately 2 feet by 3 feet area. Pressing on floor shows deflection - indicates possible water damage to subfloor from tub overflow or shower leak. Further investigation required by contractor. Recommendation: Plumber should snake guest bath drain and inspect vent stack. Contractor should open main bath floor to assess subfloor damage. Estimated cost: Guest bath drain $100-200, Main bath floor repair $500-2,000 depending on extent of damage. Priority: Main bath is medium-high due to structural concern."

**11:30 AM - Attic** (cramped, hot, dusty - no clipboard possible)
- "Attic: Accessed via hallway ceiling hatch. Attic temperature approximately 110 degrees Fahrenheit. Insulation: Blown fiberglass insulation, measured at R-19 depth. Current code for Austin climate zone requires R-38. Home is under-insulated, likely resulting in 20-30% higher cooling costs in summer. Recommendation: Add insulation to achieve R-38. Cost: $1,500-$2,500 for professional installation. Energy savings: approximately $30-50/month in peak summer. Moisture: Identified water staining on roof decking approximately 3 feet west of bathroom vent fan. Staining pattern suggests condensation or roof leak. Vent fan duct is not properly sealed - warm moist bathroom air is venting into attic instead of outside. Recommendation: Reroute or seal bath vent duct, inspect roof decking for leak, may need sheathing replacement in affected area. Cost: $300-800. Structural: Roof framing is 2x6 rafters on 16-inch centers, no signs of sagging, cracking, or insect damage. Framing is sound."

**12:00 PM - Client Walkthrough** (30 min, recorded)
- Michael reviews major findings with Sarah and Lisa (all recorded):
  - Discussion about roof replacement timeline and cost
  - Sarah's question: "How urgent is the electrical issue?" - Michael's detailed answer recorded
  - Lisa's question: "Will this affect the appraisal?" - Response captured
  - Sarah: "Can I use this report to negotiate with the seller?" - Guidance provided
- **Benefit**: Sarah has full recording of walkthrough, can review with boyfriend later

**12:30 PM - Lunch Break**
- Michael reviews AI transcript summary on iPad (10 minutes):
  - High Priority Issues: 3 items (electrical, main bath floor, roof vent)
  - Medium Priority: 4 items (roof replacement, attic insulation, guest bath drain, foundation crack)
  - Total estimated repairs: $10,500-$17,000
- AI has auto-categorized findings by: Roof, Foundation, Electrical, Plumbing, HVAC, Interior, Attic
- Feels confident all details captured accurately

**Evening (6:00 PM - 7:30 PM): Report Generation** (90 minutes)

- Adds photos to corresponding sections (photos auto-linked by timestamp)
- Reviews final 48-page report - more detailed than usual
- Sends to Sarah at 7:30 PM (same-day delivery!)
- **Accuracy**: 99% (every detail captured)
- **Quality**: Excellent - most comprehensive report Michael has ever written
- **Time**: 90 minutes (vs. 5 hours)
