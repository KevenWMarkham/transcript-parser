## ðŸ˜“ Pain Points & Frustrations

### Current Research Challenges
1. **Information Density**
   - Seminars cover cutting-edge research (very complex)
   - Speakers present math-heavy content (equations, algorithms)
   - Can't listen actively while taking detailed notes
   - Misses nuances in Q&A discussions

2. **Note Organization**
   - Has notes scattered across: notebooks, iPad, laptop, voice memos
   - Hard to find specific ideas weeks later
   - Can't remember which seminar mentioned a particular technique
   - No way to link related ideas across different talks

3. **Citation Management**
   - Speakers mention papers but Marcus can't write down citations fast enough
   - Later can't remember: "Was that Smith et al. 2023 or 2024?"
   - Wastes time hunting down papers mentioned in seminars
   - Needs accurate citations for his own papers

4. **Collaboration Friction**
   - Lab meeting decisions get forgotten or misremembered
   - Teammates ask "What did we decide about X last week?"
   - No shared record of who's doing what
   - Ideas get lost in discussion, no follow-up

### Technology Pain Points
- Uses Otter.ai but transcripts are messy (technical terms wrong)
- Takes iPad notes but they're unstructured and hard to search
- Uses Zotero for papers but can't link to seminar notes
- No good way to tag "potential research directions" across sources

## ðŸŽ“ Typical Use Cases

### Research Seminar Workflow
1. **Before Seminar**: Creates new transcript with tags (speaker, topic, conference/internal)
2. **During Seminar**: Focuses on understanding, occasional iPad sketches of equations
3. **After Seminar** (same day):
   - Reviews transcript, corrects AI errors in technical terms
   - Adds annotations: "Related to my Chapter 3" or "Potential baseline method"
   - Extracts paper citations mentioned by speaker
   - Generates "Research Ideas" section with AI assistance
4. **During Writing** (weeks/months later):
   - Searches across all seminars for specific techniques
   - Finds where he first learned about an approach
   - Cites papers that were mentioned in seminar Q&A

### Lab Meeting Workflow
1. **Records entire 2-hour lab meeting**
2. **Extracts action items** - "Marcus: run baseline experiments by Friday"
3. **Generates summary** - Shares with advisor to confirm understanding
4. **Links to project notes** - Connects decisions to project documentation
5. **Creates reminders** - Tracks commitments and deadlines

### Conference Workflow
1. **Records 10-15 talks at NeurIPS/ICLR**
2. **Tags by topic** - Attention mechanisms, multi-modal learning, etc.
3. **Cross-references papers** - Links transcripts to arXiv PDFs in Zotero
4. **Identifies collaborators** - Notes interesting researchers to follow up with
5. **Exports insights** - Creates conference summary for lab group

## ðŸŽ¨ Design Implications

### UI/UX Preferences
- **Power user features** - Keyboard shortcuts, bulk operations
- **Technical accuracy** - Custom vocabulary for ML/NLP terms
- **Integration-first** - Export to Notion, Zotero, Obsidian, Markdown
- **Flexible organization** - Tags, folders, projects, graph view
- **API access** - Wants to script workflows

### Feature Priorities (Ranked)
1. **Technical term accuracy** - Custom dictionaries for domains
2. **Citation extraction** - Auto-detect paper references
3. **Search across transcripts** - Fast, semantic search
4. **Export to research tools** - Markdown, BibTeX, Notion API
5. **Collaboration** - Shared lab meeting notes with comments
6. **Equation support** - LaTeX formatting for math
7. **Tagging & linking** - Connect related ideas across sources

### Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)
- Dumbed-down interfaces (he's technical, wants control)
- Proprietary formats (needs open data, exportability)
- No API (wants automation, custom workflows)
- Limited customization (every researcher works differently)
- Inaccurate transcription of technical content (deal-breaker)

## ðŸ”¬ Technical Requirements

### Domain-Specific Needs
- **Custom vocabularies**: Machine learning, NLP, math, statistics terms
- **Equation recognition**: Capture LaTeX or image snapshots of math
- **Code snippets**: Detect when speaker shares pseudocode or algorithms
- **Graph structures**: Visualize concept relationships over time
- **Version control**: Track how ideas evolve across meetings

### Integration Needs
- **Zotero**: Link transcripts to papers, export citations
- **Notion**: Sync notes to research wiki
- **Obsidian**: Export Markdown with internal links
- **Overleaf**: Copy sections into LaTeX papers
- **Slack**: Share meeting summaries with lab group
