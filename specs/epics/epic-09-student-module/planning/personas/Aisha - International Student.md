## üéØ Goals & Motivations

### Academic Goals
- **Graduate with honors** - Prove herself academically (family expectations)
- **Improve English proficiency** - Especially business terminology
- **Understand cultural context** - Marketing concepts tied to Western culture
- **Build professional network** - Critical for post-graduation work permit
- **Prepare for career** - Digital marketing role in Canada or international company

### Language Learning Goals
- Master business English (presentations, reports, idioms)
- Understand fast-paced lecture delivery (professors speak quickly)
- Learn cultural references (humor, case studies, brand examples)
- Reduce comprehension anxiety during lectures
- Build confidence in class participation

### Why She Needs Transcript Parser
- Records all lectures to re-listen and catch missed details
- Needs written transcripts to look up unfamiliar words
- Wants to improve pronunciation by comparing her notes to transcript
- Uses transcripts to prepare for class discussions (needs time to process)
- Translates key business terms to Bengali for better understanding

## üòì Pain Points & Frustrations

### Current Language Challenges
1. **Lecture Comprehension**
   - Professors speak fast, use idioms, make cultural references
   - Misses jokes and examples (everyone laughs, she's confused)
   - Takes longer to process information in non-native language
   - Fear of asking "dumb questions" makes her stay silent

2. **Note-Taking Difficulties**
   - Can't write notes fast enough (thinking in Bengali, writing in English)
   - Misses content while looking up unfamiliar words on phone
   - Handwriting is messy when rushing (hard to read later)
   - Doesn't know which points are important vs. just examples

3. **Cultural Context Gaps**
   - Marketing case studies reference unfamiliar brands/campaigns
   - Doesn't understand North American consumer behavior
   - Misses nuances in advertising that native students get immediately
   - Feels "behind" classmates who grew up with this context

4. **Study Inefficiency**
   - Re-listens to entire 1.5-hour lectures (takes 3-4 hours with pauses)
   - Looks up 10-15 words per lecture in dictionary
   - Struggles to create study materials (unsure what's important)
   - Anxiety about exams (worried she misunderstood concepts)

### Social & Emotional Pain Points
- Feels isolated (language barrier limits friendships)
- Imposter syndrome (thinks she's not as smart as native English speakers)
- Hesitant to form study groups (worried about slowing others down)
- Family pressure (parents sacrificed financially for her education)
- Anxiety about post-graduation work permit (needs good grades)

## üéì Typical Use Cases

### Lecture Recording & Review Workflow
1. **Before Lecture**: Starts recording (phone on desk, afraid to miss content)
2. **During Lecture**:
   - Tries to write notes but often just listens
   - Marks timestamps for confusing parts (0:32, 1:15, etc.)
   - Writes down unfamiliar words to look up later
3. **After Lecture** (same evening):
   - Re-listens to recording at 0.75x speed (makes it more understandable)
   - Pauses to look up words in Google Translate
   - Writes complete notes based on audio + rough class notes
   - Translates key concepts to Bengali for clarity
4. **Before Exam** (1-2 weeks later):
   - Re-reads notes, highlights important points
   - Creates flashcards for terminology
   - Wishes she had summaries (re-reading is overwhelming)

### With Transcript Parser - Ideal Workflow
1. **During Lecture**: Focuses on listening and understanding
2. **After Lecture** (on commute home):
   - Opens transcript on phone
   - Reads through (easier to comprehend than audio)
   - Taps unfamiliar words for instant translation/definition
   - AI highlights key concepts (so she knows what's important)
3. **At Home**:
   - Generates flashcards automatically (English + Bengali definitions)
   - Gets AI summary in simpler English
   - Exports study guide as PDF
4. **Before Exam**:
   - Reviews AI-generated quiz questions
   - Uses spaced repetition for flashcards
   - Feels confident about coverage

## üé® Design Implications

### UI/UX Preferences
- **Simple, clear language** - Avoid jargon in UI/instructions
- **Visual learning aids** - Icons, color coding, highlighting
- **Reading-first design** - Transcripts more important than audio
- **Translation integration** - Seamless word/phrase lookup
- **Mobile-optimized** - Studies primarily on phone/tablet
- **Offline mode** - Spotty internet in some study locations

### Feature Priorities (Ranked)
1. **Accurate transcription** - Foundation for everything else
2. **Translation integration** - Built-in dictionary/translation
3. **Simplified summaries** - Clearer English for non-native speakers
4. **Key concept highlighting** - Shows what's important
5. **Bilingual flashcards** - English term + definition in native language
6. **Cultural context notes** - Explains references/idioms
7. **Slow-down playback** - 0.75x or 0.5x speed option

### Accessibility Considerations
- **Text size controls** - Needs larger text on phone
- **High contrast mode** - Easier reading in bright library
- **Simple navigation** - Fewer clicks to key features
- **Error forgiveness** - Clear undo, helpful error messages
- **Help documentation** - Video tutorials, not just text

### Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)
- Complex English in UI (use simple, clear words)
- Assuming cultural knowledge (explain idioms, references)
- Audio-only features (she needs text)
- No offline mode (internet unreliable)
- Expensive pricing (student budget, international tuition already high)

## üåç International Student Considerations

### Unique Needs
- **Language learning** - Tool should aid English acquisition, not just transcribe
- **Cultural adaptation** - Help understand context, not just content
- **Budget constraints** - International tuition is 3-4x domestic rates
- **Family pressure** - High expectations, need to succeed
- **Isolation** - Technology can help connect with classmates

### Regulatory Awareness
- **Study permit compliance** - Must maintain full-time status, passing grades
- **Post-graduation work permit** - GPA affects eligibility
- **Privacy concerns** - Worried about data (family in Bangladesh)
