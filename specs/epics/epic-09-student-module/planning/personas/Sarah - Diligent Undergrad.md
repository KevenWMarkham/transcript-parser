## ðŸ˜“ Pain Points & Frustrations

### Current Study Challenges
1. **Information Overload**
   - Professors cover too much material too quickly
   - Hard to identify what's "important" for exams
   - Gets lost in details, misses big picture

2. **Note-Taking Struggles**
   - Can't write fast enough during lectures
   - Misses explanations while copying diagrams
   - Notes are messy and hard to review later

3. **Exam Preparation Time**
   - Creating flashcards manually takes 2-3 hours per lecture
   - Re-watching entire recorded lectures is time-consuming
   - Can't find specific topics quickly in 90-minute recordings

4. **Study Inefficiency**
   - Doesn't know which concepts she understands vs. needs more work
   - Wastes time re-studying material she already knows
   - Procrastinates because exam prep feels overwhelming

### Technology Pain Points
- Uses basic phone recorder app (no transcript, just audio)
- Relies on handwritten notes (hard to search, often incomplete)
- Manually creates Quizlet flashcards (very time-consuming)
- No way to track which topics she's mastered

## ðŸŽ“ Typical Use Cases

### Weekly Lecture Recording Workflow
1. **Before Lecture**: Opens Transcript Parser, starts new recording with course tag
2. **During Lecture**: Focuses on listening, lets app record + transcribe
3. **After Lecture** (within 24 hours):
   - Reviews AI-generated summary (5 min vs. 90 min re-watch)
   - Highlights key definitions and concepts
   - Generates flashcards for terminology
4. **Before Exam** (1-2 weeks later):
   - Uses search to find specific topics across all lectures
   - Reviews quiz questions generated from transcripts
   - Exports flashcards to Anki for spaced repetition

### Specific Scenarios
- **Missed class**: Gets transcript from classmate, generates own study guide
- **Lab lecture**: Records complex procedure explanation, re-watches specific steps
- **Office hours**: Records TA explaining difficult concept, creates notes
- **Study group**: Shares annotated transcript with study partners

## ðŸŽ¨ Design Implications

### UI/UX Preferences
- **Simple, clean interfaces** - No clutter, focus on content
- **Quick access to key features** - Flashcards, summaries, search
- **Visual hierarchy** - Important concepts highlighted
- **Mobile-optimized** - Big touch targets, readable text
- **Progress indicators** - Show what's complete, what needs work

### Feature Priorities (Ranked)
1. **Auto-generated flashcards** - Saves most time
2. **Lecture summaries** - Quickly review key points
3. **Search across lectures** - Find topics instantly
4. **Quiz generation** - Self-testing for retention
5. **Study guide export** - PDF for printing/annotating
6. **Spaced repetition reminders** - Better than Anki, easier to use

### Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)
- Complex setup processes (wants 1-click start)
- Too many customization options (analysis paralysis)
- Jargon or academic language in UI
- Requiring desktop app (needs mobile flexibility)
- Manual tagging/categorization (should be automatic)
